Description SayCopy turns short voice recordings into editable text you can use anywhere. Record a thought, meeting follow-up, message, or note. SayCopy transcribes it, optionally translates it, and lets you refine the result before copying or sharing it to another app. WHAT YOU CAN DO • Transcribe short voice recordings • Translate between English, Spanish, and Arabic • Apply light cleanup for punctuation, capitalization, and readability • Edit results before you copy or share • Keep searchable history on your device • Organize saved results with tags • Choose recommended or custom OpenRouter models PRIVATE BY DESIGN SayCopy does not require a SayCopy account. Your settings and history stay on your device. Your OpenRouter API key is stored in secure iOS storage. Audio and text are sent only when you request processing, with zero-data-retention routing required. BRING YOUR OWN OPENROUTER KEY SayCopy is free to download. To process audio or text, you provide your own OpenRouter API key. OpenRouter and selected model providers may charge for usage. An internet connection is required for transcription and translation. Learn how to set up a key at https://saycopy.app/setup.

Review notes

SayCopy does not have a SayCopy account or sign-in flow. To test the core flow: 1. Open Settings. 2. Enter the review OpenRouter API key supplied below and tap Save token. 3. Return to Record. 4. Choose Transcribe or Translate, then record a short clip. 5. Stop recording and wait for processing. 6. Edit the result, then test Copy, Share, Tags, and History. The app sends audio/text to OpenRouter and eligible model providers for the user-requested operation. Every processing request includes zero-data-retention routing. Local history can be deleted item-by-item. App Privacy declaration

Tracking: No.

Data used for third-party advertising: No.

Data linked to the user, App Functionality: User ID (the user-owned OpenRouter account/API credential), Audio Data, Other User Content, and Other Usage Data.

Data handling note: SayCopy does not operate a backend or account system. OpenRouter/model-provider processing is the third-party collection path. Per-request ZDR is enforced, but Audio Data and Other User Content remain disclosed conservatively because a user may separately enable input/output logging in their OpenRouter account. OpenRouter stores operational request metadata for usage reporting and billing.
